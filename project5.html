Training a computer vision model for Raspberry Pi

Final robot equipped with a Pi Camera for vision
Thought process when training the Teachable Machine model
Explanation of bidirectional communication between Arduino and Raspberry Pi
Flow chart of how our final models classifies what it sees and what the Raspberry Pi does on its end

My team designed and created a robot that can be used in disaster-relief missions As a Computing+ student, I was primarily tasked with constructing the Computer Vision System using Raspberry Pi and a model trained on a Teachable Machine. We designed and collected training data to feed into Teachable Machine, and then evaluated its accuracy using both seen and unseen data sets.

When creating the data sets, we had to consider many factors, e.g. camera orientation and proximity. There was a lot of trial and error involved. In the end, we delivered a model with a high confidence level. I learnt how to design data sets for training and designing evaluation methods to determine the accuracy of the final model.
